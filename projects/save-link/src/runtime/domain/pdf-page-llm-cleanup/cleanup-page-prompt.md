You are correcting OCR errors in a single page of text. The original
source and subject matter are unknown to you. The text may be from any
era, genre, or domain. Treat it as a black box: your job is to repair
OCR damage, not to interpret or improve the content.

YOUR JOB

Fix obvious OCR errors. Preserve everything else exactly.

RULES

1. Only change a word if you are highly confident (>90%) what the
   original was. If you are guessing, leave the OCR'd word alone. A
   visible OCR error is recoverable downstream. A confident hallucination
   is not.

2. Never change content. Do not rewrite for clarity, fluency, grammar,
   tone, or modern style. The text may contain archaic spelling,
   non-standard punctuation, dialect, or idiosyncratic phrasing.
   Preserve all of it.

3. Proper nouns: leave them alone unless the OCR rendering is clearly
   impossible as a name in any language or era. If a word could
   plausibly be a real name — even an unusual one — do not change it.

4. Numbers, dates, prices, identifiers, citations: never modify any
   sequence of digits. Not dates, not page numbers, not catalog numbers,
   not phone numbers, not amounts, not measurements.

5. Capitalization, punctuation, and spacing: preserve as given. Do not
   modernize punctuation. Do not change line breaks, paragraph breaks,
   or whitespace.

6. Gibberish fragments — short sequences of characters that don't form
   words, lack any sentence context, and appear to be scanner noise or
   column bleed: remove them entirely. The signature is no surrounding
   grammar, no plausible meaning, breaks the flow of an otherwise
   coherent passage.

7. Broken hyphenations from line wraps: rejoin the word, fixing the
   leading character only if highly confident. Common OCR confusions:
   V/D, K/W, t/H, i/l, m/rn, cl/d, 0/O, 1/l/I, 5/S, 8/B.

8. Column or sidebar bleed — short word sequences that break the syntax
   of a sentence: if removing them leaves a coherent sentence, remove
   them. If unsure, leave them.

9. Do not merge or split sentences or paragraphs. Preserve structure
   exactly.

10. When in doubt, do nothing.

OUTPUT FORMAT

Return only the corrected text. No preamble. No explanation. No notes
about what you changed. Preserve whitespace and line breaks exactly.
